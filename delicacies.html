Binallay

Binallay (Isabela) This delicacy is made from glutinous rice flour, steamed in banana leaves, and served with a sauce made from coconut milk and raw sugar. It's often enjoyed during Holy Week.

×

Chicharabao

Chicharabao is a unique Filipino snack from Cagayan, Philippines, that is made from carabao (water buffalo) skin, rather than the usual pork skin. It's a variationof the popular "chicharon," which is typically madefrom deep-fried pork belly or rind.

×

Lunis

Lunis (Batanes) this is a different version of the popular Filipino dish, Adobo because Lunis is rather served dry. Lasting for quite a while, this traditional dish is made of pork cooked with rock salt until its fat is rendered and it turns golden brown.

×

Buridibud

Buridibud (Nueva Vizcaya) is a vegetable soup dish similar to dinengdeng, the main difference is that this dish uses sweet potatoes as its main ingredient where it is cooked to the point that it’s so soft it breaks down with the soup giving it a thicker texture.

×

Pancit Cabagan

The dish is usually made with a combination of lechon carajay (crispy pork belly that's boiled with spices and deep-fried), egg noodles, quail eggs, carrots, bell peppers, parsley, pork shoulder, cabbage, garlic, beef stock, soy sauce, fish sauce, scallions, oil, and black pepper.

Pancit Pusit

Pancit Pusit, also known as Pancit Choca en su Tinta, is a signature Caviteño noodle dish known for its striking dark hue and rich, savory flavor derived from squid ink. It's a local specialty, originating from Cavite, where fresh seafood is abundant.

×

Bulalo

A staple of the Batangueno diet is Bulalo or Beef Shank Soup. Because Batangas is located on an elevated plateau, this soup is ideal for boosting your energy and keeping you warm in the chillier weather.

×

Minaluto

Minaluto dish ng Balaw-Balaw Restaurant Angono, Rizal. Minaluto is a local version of Spanish Paella. Its rice served with pork, chicken and seafood such as squid, shrimp and crabs, salted egg and various vegetables.

×

Pancit Habhab

Pancit Habhab is a signature Quezon delicacy, specifically from Lucban, served on a banana leaf and eaten directly without utensils, with its name mimicking the sound of slurping or "habhabing".

Suman sa Lihiya

Suman sa lihiya type of sticky rice cake unique to Oriental Mindoro. This delicacy is made from glutinous rice, lye water, and is wrapped in banana leaves, often served with a topping of fresh coconut and sugar or a coconut caramel sauce called latik.

×

Adobong Pugita

Adobong pugita is a Filipino specialty originating from Occidental Mindoro. This adobo dish is made with mature octopus that's cooked in a combination of vinegar and soy sauce. Other common ingredients include garlic, onions, oil, and bay leaves for flavoring.

×

Pinahiran

Similar to bibingkang malagkit of mainland Luzon, Pinahiran is another kakanin from Marinduque, particularly from Santa Cruz. Sometimes called bibingkang pinahiran, it is a large cake made from glutinous rice, coconut milk and brown sugar that looks and tastes more like biko than a typical bibingka. A sweet syrup is spread over the top of this dessert, hence its name meaning “coated” or “smeared”

×

Sarsa na Uyang

Romblon is known for Sarsa (or Sarsa na uyang), a unique delicacy made from pounded freshwater shrimp mixed with coconut, chili, and spices, then wrapped in coconut leaves and boiled in coconut milk.

×

Tamilok

Tamilok, a slimy, shell-less saltwater clam also known as the "woodworm," is a Palawan delicacy harvested from dead mangrove wood. It's typically eaten raw or marinated with vinegar, chilies, and onions, and is considered healthy and rich in vitamins.

Kinaluko

Kinaluko, a delicacy from Masbate, is a sweet treat made from carabao' milk and sugar, similar to pastillas but with a firmer texture.

×

Catandungan Laing

"Catandungan Laing" likely refers to Laing, a popular Bicolano dish, specifically associated with the province of Catanduanes. Laing is a spicy and savory dish made with taro leaves, coconut milk, chilies, and often meat or seafood. The term "Catandungan" is likely used to specify its origin or connection to Catanduanes, a province in the Bicol region.

×

Hinagom

Hinagom or Hinagum of Sorsogon recipe is simple, it’s made from freshly harvested rice that’s pounded, roasted in a pan, then pounded again in a mortar mixed with coconut meat and sugar before wrapping in leaves. It tastes quite similar to Espasol of Laguna.

Binungor

Binungor is a traditional Kalinga dish, known for its spiciness and unique combination of ingredients. It's a vegetable and snail stew featuring agurong (a type of snail), bamboo shoots, mushrooms, and chili peppers, often cooked with local vegetables and sometimes with a bit of fermented fish for added flavor.

×

Pinikpikan

Pinikpikan is a popular dish in the Mountain Province and other parts of the Cordillera region of the Philippines. It is often served at special occasions, such as weddings, festivals, and gatherings. Pinikpikan is also a symbol of the region’s rich culture and heritage

×

Pinuneg

Pinuneg is a Filipino blood sausage originating from the Igorots. It is made with pig's blood (sometimes cow's or carabao's blood), minced pork fat, salt, red onions, ginger, and garlic stuffed into a casing made from pig's small intestine. It is traditionally prepared during pig sacrifice ceremonies.

Crispy Pata

Crispy pata is one of themost common Filipino dishes served on special occasions. It consists of a whole pork leg that is cooked until tender. It is then dried and deep-fried until golden brown and crispy. The leg is usually cooked alongside various spices such as bay leaves and peppercorns.

×

Pancit

Pancit is a staple Filipino dish found at numerous feasts and celebrations, consisting of stir-fried noodles with meat and vegetables such as chicken, pork, shrimp, celery, carrots, onions, garlic, and cabbage.

×

Kare-Kare

Kare-kare is a traditional stew consisting of meat such as tripe, pork leg, ox tail, goat or chicken, vegetables, and a thick, savory peanut sauce flavored with annatto seeds. Shrimp paste (bagoong) is often served on the side in order to enhance the flavors of the dish.
